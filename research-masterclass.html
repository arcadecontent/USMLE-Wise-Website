Curriculum

Six modules. Everything you need to start.

Each module builds on the last, taking you from zero context to a working understanding of how research is designed, written, and published.

Research Basics

What makes a good research question, how studies are structured, and where your project fits in the evidence pyramid.

Study Designs

The main study types used in clinical research: their strengths, limitations, and what each type is best suited for.

Literature Searching

How to use PubMed and other databases to find, evaluate, and organize relevant literature efficiently and systematically.

Research Ethics

An introduction to IRB requirements, informed consent, ICMJE authorship standards, and what publication integrity actually means.

Scientific Writing

The structure of a research manuscript, how to write each section clearly, and the difference between first-draft and submission-ready quality.

Journal Submission

How journals work, what editors look for, how to choose the right journal, and what the peer review process looks like from the author's side.
