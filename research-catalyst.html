Why research stays on the to-do list

The four walls most IMGs hit.

01

The mentorship gap

Top researchers are too busy to teach. Those with time often lack the high-impact experience you need to learn from.

02

The free labor trap

Unpaid observer positions come with zero guidance. You do basic tasks with no clear path to a meaningful publication.

03

Scattered effort, no output

Without a clear strategy, projects start but don't finish. Months pass with nothing on the CV to show for it.

04

Skills no one taught you

Medical school doesn't cover study design, scientific writing, or journal submission. You're left to figure it out on your own.

Publication Types

Case Reports

The fastest route to publication. You identify an interesting case, learn to write it up, and get a strong CV line that shows clinical curiosity.

Narrative Reviews

Develop expertise in a niche topic by synthesizing existing literature into a comprehensive review that positions you as a knowledgeable applicant.

Retrospective Studies

Learn study design and data analysis by working with existing datasets. A foundational skill that academic programs actively look for.

Prospective Studies

Participate in a study from the ground up. Your mentor guides design, IRB process, data collection, and analysis in real time.

How it works

Six stages from assessment to publication.

Stage 01

Research Assessment

We evaluate your academic background, specialty interests, and residency goals to build a personalized research roadmap.

Stage 02

Topic Selection

We identify impactful, feasible research projects aligned with your target specialty and timeline.

Stage 03

Training & Development

You learn research methodology, literature review, scientific writing, and publication ethics with your mentor.

Stage 04

Project Execution

You conduct the research alongside your mentor — gathering data, analyzing results, and building the manuscript foundation.

Stage 05

Manuscript Preparation

Your mentor guides you through drafting, revising, and finalizing a publication-ready manuscript.

Stage 06

Submission & Publication

We select the right journals, guide you through submission, and support you through reviewer responses until your work is published.
