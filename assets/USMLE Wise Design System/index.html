01 — Foundation

Brand Overview

USMLE Wise guides international medical graduates through the USMLE and the Match — the long, ambiguous journey of becoming a US physician. Our brand exists to make that journey feel knowable.

MISSION

To turn the most overwhelming process in medical education into a clear, walkable path — with a mentor at every step.

PROMISES
  • We know the process. Every step, every form, every deadline.
  • We know how to get the outcome. Match-ready, residency-bound.
  • You are not alone. US physician mentors, real students like you.
  • Beginner or advanced — we will guide you. Meet you where you are.

02 — Foundation

Brand Personality

Calm confidence. We are the senior physician sitting across the desk who has done this 100 times before — patient, prepared, and certain you'll get there.

Voice attributes

DEFINING TRAIT
Certain & Competent
We speak with the authority of physicians who have actually walked this path. No hedging on what matters — but no arrogance about what doesn't.
Friendly & Welcoming
Medicine is intimidating enough. We greet students the way a good attending greets a new resident.
Clear & Structured
No jargon for jargon's sake. We turn ambiguity into checklists, milestones, and next steps.
Human & Calm
We never amplify anxiety. The tone is measured, encouraging, never urgent without reason.
Academic & Precise
When we talk about content, we are exact. Sources cited, ranges given, edge cases noted.
Motivational & PatientProgress over pressure. We celebrate effort and stamina, not just scores. A student who needed two attempts is not a failure — they are a physician who learned to persist.

Voice — do & don't

Do · "We know the process." "Step 1 is changing in 2027. Here's what stays the same and what your study plan should adjust — we'll walk through it together."
Don't · "We're disruptive." "Crush Step 1 with our AI-powered next-gen revolutionary platform 🚀 — students are LOVING it!!!"

SECONDARY

INFLUENCERS

Color usage rules

The 60·30·10 rule60% neutral surfaces (white, paper, ink-75) · 30% USMLE Blue for structure and trust · 10% Wise Red exclusively for CTAs and energy moments.
Red is preciousNever use Wise Red as a large background, body text color, or for routine UI chrome. Reserve it for primary CTAs, key statistics, and one accent moment per screen.
CTA red ≠ Danger redWise Red (#E32124) is for action — buttons, conversion moments, energy. Danger (#B91C1C) is for errors, destructive states, and alerts. Using the same color for both trains users to distrust the interface. Never share a hex between these two roles.

Educational UI

Patterns specific to learning: questions, explanations, flashcards, lessons. Every educational surface follows the same shape: stem · choices · explanation · references.

QBank question

Cardiology Step 2 CK High yield
Q 42 of 80 · 01:24

A 64-year-old man presents with substernal chest pain that began 90 minutes ago while shoveling snow. Pain radiates to the left jaw and is associated with diaphoresis and nausea. He has a history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes. On exam, BP is 162/94, HR 98, SpO₂ 97%. ECG shows 2 mm ST-segment elevation in leads II, III, and aVF. Which of the following is the most appropriate next step?

Explanation panel

Correct — B
Inferior STEMI · time-to-PCI 90 min

ST-elevation in II, III, and aVF identifies an inferior wall MI, most commonly involving the right coronary artery. The clear time of symptom onset and the diagnostic ECG meet criteria for emergent reperfusion. PCI is preferred over fibrinolytics when door-to-balloon < 90 min is achievable.

PearlAlways check a right-sided V4R lead in inferior STEMI — RV involvement changes management (avoid nitrates).

20 — Visual Language

Motion & Animation

Motion should feel like a hand guiding a page — never bouncy, never showy. Three durations, two easings, one principle: motion exists to explain change, not to entertain.

Durations & easings

fast · 140 ms · ease-out
Hover, focus, micro-state changes
medium · 240 ms · ease-out
Dropdowns, tooltips, card lifts
slow · 420 ms · ease-in-out
Modal entry, page transitions, progress fills

Hover any tile above to preview the curve. All motion respects prefers-reduced-motion; under that media query, durations collapse to 0 ms.

Loading states

SKELETON
SPINNER
Indeterminate · 0.9s
PROGRESS
Determinate · animates on value change

Motion principles

PurposefulEvery animation explains a change in state. If you can't say what changed, it shouldn't move.
CalmDefault to ease-out. We never overshoot, never bounce, never spring. Students are anxious enough.
ConsistentThree durations and two curves cover the entire product. Anything else needs a written justification.
AccessibleAll motion under 5 seconds, never auto-loops in the periphery, fully disabled at reduced-motion.

Accessibility

Our students study for 8-hour blocks. The interface has to hold up under tired eyes, on poor connections, in second languages. WCAG 2.2 AA is the floor, not the ceiling.

Contrast — verified pairings

12.4 : 1 · AAA
Red 500 on White
5.2 : 1 · AA
Ink 900 on White

Operating principles

Color is never the only signalStatus badges always pair with a dot, icon, or label. A correct answer is green AND a check mark AND the word "Correct".
Focus is always visibleEvery interactive element has a 2px outset focus ring at 3:1 contrast minimum. Never outline:none without a replacement.
Minimum target: 44 × 44 pxTouch targets meet WCAG 2.2 AA. Phone navigation, answer choices, and primary CTAs are 48 px on mobile.
Reading line lengthBody copy is constrained to 56–75 characters per line. Forms cap input width to 480 px so the eye doesn't have to track.
Cognitive loadOne primary action per screen. Onboarding flows split into max-4-step progressive disclosure. Save state continuously, never lose work.
Long-session usabilityDefault to system font scaling. Dark mode planned for v1.1. Auto-pauses video after 30 min of no interaction.

Developer Handoff

Practical notes for the engineering team. The system ships as plain CSS variables today. A token JSON / Tailwind config is on the v1.1 roadmap.

Using the system

/* Import the stylesheet once at the app root */
@import "styles.css";

/* Then use tokens — never hex values directly */
.cta {
  background: var(--uw-red-500);
  color: #fff;
  padding: var(--space-3) var(--space-5);
  border-radius: var(--r-md);
  font-family: var(--font-sans);
  transition: background var(--dur-fast) var(--ease-out);
}
.cta:hover { background: var(--uw-red-600); }

Component naming
