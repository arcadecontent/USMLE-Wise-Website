Background

Strong scores. No U.S. exposure. One failed cycle.

Ahmed graduated from Aga Khan University with two years of research experience and Step scores most IMG applicants would consider competitive: 232 on Step 1, 248 on Step 2 CK. On paper, the profile looked solid. In practice, there was a critical gap: no U.S. clinical experience, and one unmatched cycle already behind him.

He reached out to USMLE Wise after his first cycle ended without a single interview invitation. At that point, the question wasn't whether his scores were good enough. It was why a competitive application was producing no results, and what specifically needed to change.

The Challenge

The application wasn't telling a coherent story.

After reviewing the full prior application, the issue became clear quickly. The program list had been built without a strategy: too many reach programs, no geographic focus, no signal usage, and a significant gap year that the personal statement worked around rather than addressed directly.

The personal statement itself buried the most compelling parts of Ahmed's background. The research, the clinical experience, the motivation for internal medicine — all present, but flattened into a list rather than shaped into a narrative. Reviewers had no reason to keep reading.

Without USCE, programs that screened on U.S. clinical experience were filtering him out automatically. The application was reaching programs that had already eliminated it before anyone read a word.

"I had the scores. I just didn't understand why none of it was converting. USMLE Wise looked at the actual application and immediately identified things I hadn't even considered."

Dr. Ahmed A.  ·  PGY-2 Internal Medicine
What We Did

Observership first. Then rebuild the application around it.

The first priority was USCE. We connected Ahmed with a 4-week observership opportunity in Cleveland that could be completed before ERAS opened in the fall. This gave the application a U.S. clinical anchor and removed him from the automatic filter at many programs.

We rewrote the personal statement from scratch. Instead of a chronological summary, the new version opened with a specific patient case from his research that illustrated why he chose internal medicine, then built the rest of the narrative around that. The gap year was addressed in the second paragraph, directly and briefly, before moving on. Acknowledging it without dwelling on it turned a perceived weakness into evidence of self-awareness.

The program list was rebuilt entirely. We identified 68 programs with documented IMG match histories, organized them into tiers, and mapped out which required USMLE signals. Ahmed applied signals to 15 programs in the early window, targeting community programs in Ohio and Pennsylvania where his score profile was a strong fit.

The Result

14 interview invitations. Matched on the first cycle with us.

Ahmed received 14 interview invitations from programs across Ohio, Pennsylvania, and New York. He accepted 11 and ranked 8. He matched at his fourth-ranked program, a categorical Internal Medicine position at a community hospital in Ohio with a strong IMG track record and a fellowship placement rate he was specifically interested in.
