# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

International Medical Graduates (IMGs) pursuing U.S. residency. They are trained physicians — often years into a demanding process — who feel overwhelmed navigating a system that was not built with them in mind. They are studying for USMLE exams, chasing rotations, building applications, and trying not to make a costly mistake. They are highly educated but operating without a map. When they land on this site, they are equal parts hopeful and exhausted. The product app (planned) will serve the same users once they become active clients: tracking progress, accessing mentors, and following their personalized roadmap.

## Product Purpose

USMLE Wise helps IMGs move from confusion to matched residency through expert mentorship, a personalized roadmap, and end-to-end support across exams, rotations, ERAS applications, and interview prep. It exists because the U.S. residency system is opaque and unforgiving — and most IMGs are navigating it alone. Success means an IMG feeling confident they have a clear plan and a knowledgeable partner who has done this before.

## Brand Personality

Warm, precise, trustworthy. The voice of a senior attending who has been through the Match, who respects your intelligence, tells you exactly what to do, and genuinely wants you to succeed. Not a salesperson. Not a hospital. A mentor.

## Anti-references

- **Overly corporate**: navy-and-gold consulting aesthetic, stiff hierarchy, impersonal copy. USMLE Wise is a mentor, not McKinsey.
- **Old-school medical**: white-and-teal, stock-photo doctors, dated hospital-website formality. This is not a clinic.
- **Generic SaaS blue**: HubSpot / Intercom rounded-card feel, feature grids, tech-blue color reflexes. This is not a B2B tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned authority**: Trust is built through specificity — real names, real Step scores, real match stories. Never make claims the content doesn't prove.
2. **Clarity under pressure**: IMGs are overwhelmed. Every screen should reduce cognitive load, not add to it. One clear next action per section.
3. **The mentor's voice**: Design and copy should feel like guidance from someone who knows the path, not a pitch from someone selling something. Warm, direct, never hypey.
4. **One continuous journey**: From landing page to future product app, the visual system should feel like a single environment that grows with the user — not a marketing site that hands off to a different product.
5. **Show, don't assure**: Use real outcomes (score improvements, match confirmation, specific programs) rather than vague assurances. The design makes space for evidence, not adjectives.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort WCAG 2.1 AA. The audience spans many languages and backgrounds — keep reading level clear, avoid jargon where possible, and ensure sufficient color contrast on all text. Reduced-motion preference is already implemented and should be preserved across all future work.
